How we help

We focus on providing these children with basic needs with a particular emphasis on education Our aim is to support each needy child so that they can be stable and happy and also to reach a stage where they are self-sufficient.
We aim to provide a safe and secure environment for the children to grow up in. We also focus on enabling each child to come to a true knowledge of God, His Word and to have a personal relationship with Him. We believe that this is the only way that they can be wholesome individuals, at peace with themselves and of use to the society as an upright, humane and responsible citizen.

Current programmes we have:

Residential care

Under our roof we house a maximum of 35 children in onsite dormitories where essential needs of food, safety, medical care, counselling and education are available to them. The children are also given daily devotions. Most complete high school before reaching 18 years of age at which they are relocted back to relatives or guardians. Some may require tertiary education assistance, depending on their academic performance. Each residential child has a sponsor sourced from Australia, to cater for their needs.

Education

We operate a registered primary school incorporating the Competency Based Curriculum (CBC), from grade 1 to 9, with a maximum of 36 students per class. A basic school fee is charged albeit at a subsidized rate by sponsors in Australia in assistance to the children in need.

Extra School Sponsorship Fund (ESSF)

Assistance is provided for up to 50 children to the nearest residential secondary schools for their Senior education . They also attend compulsory Christian counselling sessions to help them cope with the life challenges they face and to encourage them to have a true relationship with God. Assistance is given for the four years of secondary education with the unfortunately rare case of continuing the assistance to tertiary level.

Counselling and Evangelism

We have an onsite church that runs various activities each week and our pastor who is also our director is involved in regular counselling, assisted by our resident social worker .The primary school activities include daily devotions, a weeky kid's club, teenage counselling and Vacation Bible School (VBS) during the school holidays. Youth conferences are also held every two years and Family Life seminars are held occasionally for parents and guardians.
